# Journal: 2026-06-09

Ironing out Phase 2 before moving on: a methodology audit to check that the identifiability verdicts
are real and not artifacts of the tools, rather than adding new scope.

## What was done

- Added `experiments/02_identifiability/verify_methodology.py`, five checks each targeting a way the
  Phase 2 results could be misleading. Output in `results/verify_methodology_summary.txt`.
- Hardened `make_dimensional_simulator` to return a large finite sentinel when the stiff solver fails
  at extreme parameters (which the optimizer probes at high noise), so fitting does not crash. The
  full test suite still passes.

## What the audit found

1. Spectral gap: the non-identifiable Fisher eigenvalue is ~1e-16 or exactly 0 relative to the largest,
   the smallest identifiable one ~1e-5 to 1e-4. The rank is a clear call.
2. The protein-only null direction aligns with the analytic k_m/k_p direction to |dot| = 1.0000 across
   finite-difference step sizes; it is the analytic degeneracy, not numerical noise.
3. Fisher standard error and profile-likelihood interval agree in order and direction. The profile is
   about 1.3 to 1.5 times the Gaussian (1.96 x SE) prediction, slightly wider as expected from
   non-Gaussian tails; the profile half-widths are grid-quantized (0.2 log units), so the agreement is
   order-of-magnitude.
4. MLE recovery: both channels recover all five parameters (max log error 0.07); protein only recovers
   the k_m k_p product (0.06) but not the split (0.50) and still recovers kappa_P (0.08). The degeneracy
   is reproduced operationally.
5. Noise sweep (M3): k_m is non-identifiable at every noise level (structural); kappa_P crosses from
   identifiable to non-identifiable between 1% and 3% noise under protein-only and between 3% and 10%
   with mRNA, so the mRNA channel buys about a decade of noise tolerance for kappa.

Net: the structural conclusion is corroborated three independent ways (analytic scaling symmetry,
Fisher rank, MLE recovery); the practical conclusion is consistent across Fisher SE, profile width, and
the noise sweep, with the quantitative noise thresholds approximate (single realization, coarse grid).

## Phase 3 and Phase 4 (initial experiments, same day)

Ran three experiments in parallel (Phase 2 closure, Phase 3, Phase 4), then verified backward.

- Phase 2 closure (`experiments/02_identifiability`): M2 and M3 both show k_m flat under protein-only
  and identifiable with mRNA. Under protein-only, kappa is only marginally identifiable for both: over
  10 noise draws the profile reads identifiable in 6/10 (M3 kappa_P) and 5/10 (M2 kappa_M), near a coin
  flip (see backward verification below).
- Phase 3 (`experiments/03_inference`): recovery (M3) - both channels recover all parameters (k_m to
  0.017 log units at 1% noise); protein-only recovers the k_m k_p product (0.030) but not the split
  (0.216). MCMC posterior (M1) - protein-only is a ridge along log k_m + log k_p = const (correlation
  -0.93, spread along the ridge 5x the spread across it); mRNA closes it into a tight blob
  (`results/fig05_posterior_ridge.png`). A fourth independent confirmation of the degeneracy after the
  analytic symmetry, Fisher rank, and MLE recovery.
- Phase 4 (`experiments/04_discrimination`): AIC/BIC discrimination. mRNA cannot separate M1 from M3
  (parsimony picks M1); the joint mRNA+protein fit separates models where a single channel cannot;
  M2 vs M3 is asymmetric (mRNA rejects M3 when the data come from M2 by delta-AIC ~1000, but M2 can
  mimic M3 when the data come from M3). Large delta-AIC robust; small ones are single-realization and
  need replication.

## Backward verification (the point of this round)

- Caught a false "non-identifiable" verdict for M2 kappa_M under protein-only: the profile scan window
  was too narrow, and on inspection the verdict is realization-dependent (borderline), not a clean
  result. Widened the window and quantified the practical identifiability of kappa over noise draws
  rather than trusting one profile. The structural Fisher result (kappa identifiable) stands; the
  practical verdict for kappa under protein-only is marginal for both models (identifiable in 6/10 and
  5/10 noise draws), not the clean split a single run suggested.
- The Phase 4 borderline cells (delta-AIC < 3) are flagged as single-realization, not quantitative.
