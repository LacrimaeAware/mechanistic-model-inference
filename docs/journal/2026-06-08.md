## What was done

Literature
- The three PDFs in `data/` were identified, moved to `data/raw` (gitignored, copyrighted), and text
  extracted. The first target (Ryzowicz and Yildirim 2023) is the 31-page author manuscript; its
  equations were transcribed and confirmed against rendered PDF pages.
- Corrections to `docs/literature.md`, verified via Crossref and PubMed: the textbook chapter title is
  "Bistability...", not "Stability..."; the two related autoregulation papers are by Murray and
  colleagues, not the first target's author, and one is 2021 (Biomolecules), not 2023. A 2025 paper
  (Yildirim, Brew and Ay, In Silico Biology) was added. The 2025 adaptation paper (Yildirim, IFFL/NFBL)
  is held locally and recorded as the Phase 5 second-target candidate.

Phase 1 (reproduction)
- The three models are in `mechanistic_inference.models` as the paper's dimensionless system
  (Eqs. 4-7). Two equation details were resolved by reading the PDF: the Hill argument is `(kappa P)^n`,
  and the input signal scales transcription as `(1 + S_a) F(p)`.
- Deterministic behavior reproduced: M1 matches its closed form (Eq. 8) to 3e-8; damped oscillation
  only in M2; response and return faster under regulation (tau_h 13.2 / 3.6 / 4.1; tau_r 48.4 / 9.2 /
  11.4 for M1 / M2 / M3).
- Stochastic behavior reproduced with an exact SSA: protein-CV ordering M2 > M1 > M3
  (0.153 / 0.141 / 0.091; paper 0.16 / 0.14 / 0.10). Caveat: the Hill-in-propensity formulation is a
  modeling choice the paper does not write out, so the exact CVs are not claimed identical.

Phase 2 (identifiability)
- Structural map: in all three models, protein-only observation leaves the transcription/translation
  rate product (k_m vs k_p) non-identifiable; the regulation strength kappa is identifiable; observing
  mRNA restores full rank. The non-identifiable direction is the same scaling symmetry in every model.
- A hypothesis that transcriptional feedback (M2) would break this degeneracy was refuted, both
  analytically (the scaling k_m c, k_p / c leaves the protein trajectory invariant) and numerically
  (the Fisher null direction is unchanged).
- Practical profiles on M3 at 3% noise: k_m flat under protein-only, bounded with mRNA; kappa_P bounded
  under both schemes but with a shallow profile, so a wide interval.
